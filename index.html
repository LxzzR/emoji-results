Bootcamp Emoji System

Summary

My cohort is a resilient and diverse group of ambitious code nerds. We were one of the first Juno College cohorts affected by covid and our entire curriculum was moved online unexpectedly – but we weren't going to let that stop us! We were all there to change our lives and¬†we were ready to work hard. I couldn't be more proud to have graduated alongside them.

Challenge

Learning to code is tough, really tough, and Bootcamp is a pretty intense way to learn to code! Not to mention in the middle of a pandemic where people's personal lives are also being deeply affected.

There was a core group of us who were quite vocal about being available for anyone who might need peer support. Despite our efforts, we discovered through an in-class survey that many of our peers were not only struggling but suffering in silence and feeling unsure who to reach out to.

"We are in this together! We're here for you!" came our usual chorus. But clearly, our approach wasn't working for those who really needed it.

Solution

I realized that our broad invitations for support might have been too vague for those struggling to ask for help. How could we be more explicit so our peers would know these offers were sincere?

I came up with an emoji system and presented it to the class the next day. Students could add an emoji to their Slack name to indicate their current availability to provide emotional support. You'll never have to worry that you're bothering someone!

Results

After graduation, I created a google survey to get feedback on the success of the emoji support system. 12 people responded, with mixed numbers of responses per question. I got some great suggestions, like how more leadership from the instructors may have been helpful in cultivating more buy-in.

Most interesting to me was that only 12% of people felt that they were able to provide the kind of support peers were seeking from them, but 100% of respondents said that their peers excelled at providing them with the support they needed!

  • 73% of respondents used the system to seek support
I only have positive things to say about this system, it is helpful and creates a platform where we can open doors and offer our help without having to worry if we are intruding on others. There is very little improve upon and those who offered their assistance were very helpful and kind.
I think sharing the roles of support giver and seeker actually ends up helping both parties more than they realize. I was reached out to by someone seeking support, and after I offered it, I ended up opening up about something that I ended up feeling like I needed support on.
